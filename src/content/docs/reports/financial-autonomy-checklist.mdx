## The Financial Autonomy Checklist

Use this checklist to track your progress toward financial autonomy. Complete each level before moving to the next.

---

## Level 1: Foundation (Weeks 1-4)

**Goal:** Stop the bleeding, establish basics

- [ ] **Track spending for 30 days** — Know where your money goes
- [ ] **List all debts** — Balances, interest rates, minimum payments
- [ ] **List all accounts** — Checking, savings, retirement, investment
- [ ] **Set up a budget** — Any system that works for you (50/30/20, zero-based, envelope)
- [ ] **Automate bill payments** — Never pay a late fee again
- [ ] **Check credit report** — Free at annualcreditreport.com

<Aside type="tip" title="Key Milestone">
You understand your complete financial picture. No surprises.
</Aside>

---

## Level 2: Stability (Months 1-3)

**Goal:** Build basic safety net, capture free money

- [ ] **Build $1,000 starter emergency fund** — Before aggressive debt payoff
- [ ] **Contribute to 401(k) up to employer match** — This is free money
- [ ] **Open high-yield savings account** — 4-5% APY instead of 0.01%
- [ ] **Set up automatic savings transfer** — Pay yourself first
- [ ] **Review all subscriptions** — Cancel unused services
- [ ] **Negotiate one bill** — Phone, internet, insurance

<Aside type="tip" title="Key Milestone">
You have $1,000 emergency cushion and are getting full employer 401(k) match.
</Aside>

---

## Level 3: Debt Freedom (Months 3-24)

**Goal:** Eliminate high-interest debt

- [ ] **Choose debt payoff method** — [Avalanche or Snowball](/protocol/debt-strategies/)
- [ ] **Automate extra debt payments** — Beyond minimums
- [ ] **Stop accumulating new debt** — Cut up cards if needed
- [ ] **Track payoff progress monthly** — Celebrate wins
- [ ] **Pay off all credit card debt** — Highest priority
- [ ] **Pay off all consumer debt** — Cars, personal loans
- [ ] **Decide on student loans/mortgage** — May not need to rush these

<Aside type="tip" title="Key Milestone">
Zero high-interest debt. Only low-rate debt remaining (if any).
</Aside>

---

## Level 4: Security (Months 6-18)

**Goal:** Full emergency fund, proper protection

- [ ] **Build full emergency fund** — 3-6 months of expenses
- [ ] **Review insurance coverage** — Health, auto, renters/home
- [ ] **Consider term life insurance** — If you have dependents
- [ ] **Consider disability insurance** — Protects your income
- [ ] **Write or update will** — Basic estate planning
- [ ] **Set up beneficiaries** — On all accounts
- [ ] **Secure important documents** — Digital backup + physical copies

<Aside type="tip" title="Key Milestone">
You can handle most financial emergencies without going into debt.
</Aside>

---

## Level 5: Wealth Building (Year 1+)

**Goal:** Maximize tax-advantaged investing

- [ ] **Max out HSA** — If eligible ($4,150 individual / $8,300 family in 2024)
- [ ] **Max out Roth IRA** — $7,000/year (2024)
- [ ] **Increase 401(k) to 15%+** — Work toward max ($23,000 in 2024)
- [ ] **Choose simple investment strategy** — [Target-date fund or 3-fund portfolio](/protocol/investment-allocation/)
- [ ] **Automate all contributions** — Set and forget
- [ ] **Rebalance annually** — Quick portfolio check
- [ ] **Start I-Bond ladder** — $10,000/year for [inflation protection](/field-notes/inflation-strategies/)

<Aside type="tip" title="Key Milestone">
All tax-advantaged space being used. Investments on autopilot.
</Aside>

---

## Level 6: Acceleration (Year 2+)

**Goal:** Beyond the basics

- [ ] **Open taxable brokerage** — After maxing tax-advantaged
- [ ] **Consider backdoor Roth** — If income exceeds limits
- [ ] **Explore mega backdoor Roth** — If 401(k) allows
- [ ] **Review career trajectory** — [Negotiate salary](/field-notes/negotiation-tactics/) or job hop
- [ ] **Develop additional income stream** — [Side income or investments](/protocol/income-streams/)
- [ ] **Calculate FI number** — 25x annual expenses
- [ ] **Track net worth quarterly** — Watch the compound growth

<Aside type="tip" title="Key Milestone">
You're optimizing. The basics are handled. Now you're accelerating.
</Aside>

---

## Level 7: Financial Independence (Years 10-25)

**Goal:** Work becomes optional

- [ ] **Reach 25x expenses invested** — The 4% rule target
- [ ] **Stress-test your plan** — Model different scenarios
- [ ] **Develop withdrawal strategy** — Tax-efficient drawdown
- [ ] **Consider healthcare** — Plan for pre-Medicare years
- [ ] **Decide on "one more year"** — Or take the leap
- [ ] **Design post-FI life** — What will you do with freedom?

<Aside type="tip" title="Key Milestone">
Investment income can cover expenses indefinitely. Work is a choice.
</Aside>
